Social Media Responsible Use Checklist
✅ The Do's
Do set your privacy settings strictly: Regularly audit your settings to ensure only friends and family can see personal posts.

Do think before you post: Ask yourself if you’d be comfortable with a professor or a future hiring manager seeing the content.

Do use social media for networking: Build a professional presence on platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni and industry leaders.

Do give credit where it's due: If you share someone else’s work, art, or ideas, make sure to tag or cite the original creator.

Do engage in positive discourse: Use your platform to support causes you care about or share academic achievements and projects.

❌ The Don'ts
Don't post illegal or policy-violating content: Avoid photos or text involving underage drinking, illicit substances, or anything that violates your college’s code of conduct.

Don't vent about professors or internships: Digital footprints are permanent; complaining about a "boring class" or a "bad boss" can come back to haunt you.

Don't overshare personal details: Keep your home address, current precise location, and class schedule off public feeds for your safety.

Don't engage with trolls or cyberbullies: Avoid heated public arguments that could make you appear unprofessional or reactive.

Don't forget that "deleted" isn't always gone: Assume that everything you post could be screenshotted or archived by someone else.
