Case Study: Cyberbullying and Online Harassment

Overview :

This case study explores a realistic scenario of Cyberbullying, specifically involving "Doxing" and "Harassment" via social media. Cyberbullying involves the use of digital platforms to intentionally and repeatedly target an individual with hostile, threatening, or shaming content.

How it Happens?

The Trigger: A student shares an opinion on a sensitive or trending topic in a college-wide WhatsApp group or on a public Instagram post.

The Escalation: A group of individuals takes offense and begins responding with personal insults rather than addressing the topic.

Doxing: The bullies find the student's personal information, such as their home address, phone number, and private family photos, and share them publicly to incite fear.

Impersonation: The perpetrators create a "fake profile" using the victim's name and photos, posting offensive or inappropriate content to ruin the victim's reputation among peers and faculty.

The Siege: The victim is bombarded with hundreds of hateful messages and "trolling" across all their social platforms, leading to digital isolation.

Target Audience:

College students in India are primary targets due to their high level of social media activity and the interconnected nature of campus life. Disagreements in person or online can quickly spiral into organized digital harassment or "cancel culture" within a university ecosystem.

Consequences:

The consequences are severe and psychological. Victims often experience extreme anxiety, depression, and a loss of self-esteem, which can lead to academic withdrawal or even self-harm. In India, cyberbullying is a punishable offense. Under the Information Technology Act (Section 66E, 67) and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) (formerly sections of the IPC), perpetrators can face strict legal action, including heavy fines and imprisonment for up to three years. Academically, students found guilty often face immediate suspension or permanent expulsion from their institutions.
